Best Free Photoshop Alternatives in 2026

Now Free!

1. Affinity Photo 2 — Best Desktop Alternative

Affinity Photo 2 was already considered the closest thing to Photoshop at a one-time price. In 2024, Canva acquired Serif (Affinity’s parent company) and made the entire Affinity suite completely free for everyone — no subscription, no watermark, no trial limit.

You get full layer support with blend modes and masks, non-destructive adjustment layers, RAW development, focus stacking, HDR merge, frequency separation, and 32-bit HDR editing.

Best for: photographers and designers who want a Photoshop replacement on Windows or macOS.

✓ Pros: PSD compatible, full feature set, no subscription
✗ Cons: Requires download; steeper learning curve
Browser

2. Photopea — Best Online Photoshop Alternative

Photopea runs in any modern browser, opens .PSD files natively, and mirrors Photoshop’s interface almost exactly — layers panel, toolbar positions, keyboard shortcuts. Works on Chromebooks and Linux with zero install.

Free with ads; Pro tier ($5/month) removes them. For most users the free version is more than enough.

Best for: anyone who works on a Chromebook or just doesn’t want to install software.

✓ Pros: PSD support, zero install, works on any device
✗ Cons: Ads in free tier; slower on large files
Open Source

3. GIMP — Most Powerful Free Desktop Editor

GIMP has been the go-to free Photoshop alternative for over 25 years. Fully open-source, runs on Windows, macOS, and Linux, with layers, curves, levels, healing, cloning, batch processing via Script-Fu, and RAW editing via the darktable plug-in.

Best for: users who need deep control over photo editing and don’t mind a learning curve.

✓ Pros: No limits, extensible with plugins, multi-platform
✗ Cons: Dated UI; limited native PSD layer support
Browser

4. Pixlr — Best for Beginners Online

Pixlr offers two browser tools: Pixlr X (simple, design-focused) and Pixlr E (layer-based, closer to Photoshop). Both are free with ads and include AI-powered background removal and generative fill in 2026.

Best for: casual users who want a polished browser editor without PSD-level complexity.

✓ Pros: Clean UI, AI tools in free tier, no install
✗ Cons: Free tier shows ads; AI features capped
Free

5. Krita — Best for Digital Artists

Krita is a professional open-source painting app that doubles as a photo editor. 100+ customizable brush engines, stabilizers, PSD support, and GPU acceleration — purpose-built for illustration and concept art.

Best for: illustrators and digital artists who also need photo compositing.

✓ Pros: Best brush engine among free tools, GPU accelerated
✗ Cons: Photography workflow is secondary to illustration
